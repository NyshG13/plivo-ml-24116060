Division of Labor: Human vs. Coding Agent

Human Role (Strategic Directives & Diagnosis)

  • Budget Constraint Identification: Recognized that standard Transformer best practices fail under a 2,000-step constraint.
  • Root-Cause Analysis: Diagnosed why AdamW, warmup schedules, and weight decay hurt performance (learning rate spent warming up during 10% of total training budget).
  • Sequence Bottleneck Realization: Identified UTF-8 Devanagari multi-byte inefficiency (3 bytes/character) as the core information bottleneck.
  • Hypothesis Generation: Directed the creation of a hybrid CharByteTokenizer to compress sequence lengths directly.

Coding Agent Role (Implementation & Execution)

  • Rapid Prototyping: Built and iterated optimizer configurations, LR schedules, and architectural tweaks within train.py.
  • Custom Tokenizer Engine: Implemented CharByteTokenizer using a 256-byte base vocabulary extended with corpus-frequent Devanagari characters.
  • Validation & JSON Handling: Structured standard load/save interfaces (char_vocab.json) maintaining fallback logic for out-of-vocab bytes.
  • Data & Pipeline Verification: Measured evaluation tokens, parameter counts, and validation BPB outputs accurately.

Why This Approach Beats the Status Quo

1. Resolving the 2,000-Step Optimization Trap:

Standard practices (cosine warmup over thousands of steps, aggressive weight decay, dropout) assume an abundance of training compute and tokens. Under a strict 2,000-step budget, these optimizations starve the network: warming up over 200 steps wastes 10% of total gradient updates on minimal progress. Eliminating aggressive regularization allowed maximum gradient utilization.

2. Tackling the True Bottleneck (Tokenizer vs. Architecture):

Devanagari characters require 3 bytes in UTF-8 byte-level tokenization. In a fixed 128-token context window, a pure byte-level tokenizer presents the model with very short context windows of actual Hindi text. The custom CharByteTokenizer assigned single token IDs to the 560 most frequent multi-byte Devanagari characters, compressing the corpus by ~22% and allowing the transformer to process significantly more textual semantic context per step.

3. Empirical Validation of Tokenizer Optimization:

By switching the information channel rather than tuning the optimizer, the Bits Per Byte dropped from 2.3718 to 2.3136 while remaining well under model parameter caps.
