Division of Labor: Human vs. Coding Agent

Human Contribution (Strategy & Diagnosis)

  • Optimization Constraint Analysis: Identified that standard practices (warmup, cosine schedules, dropout) waste crucial steps under a strict 2,000-step limit.
  • Sequence Bottleneck Identification: Recognized that Devanagari UTF-8 multi-byte encoding (3 bytes per character) was severely restricting the effective context seen per step.
  • Hypothesis Generation: Directed the strategy away from hyperparameter/architectural scaling and towards sequence compression via custom tokenization.

Coding Agent Contribution (Execution & Implementation)

  • Tokenizer Engineering: Implemented CharByteTokenizer in tokenizer.py with byte-fallback and frequent character mapping.
  • Integration & Pipeline Updates: Handled proper JSON saving/loading (char_vocab.json) and modified train.py and evaluate.py without breaking baseline requirements.
  • Data & Metrics Verification: Ran training loops, monitored metric evaluations, and structured standard evaluation logs.

Why This Solution Beats the Status Quo

1. Eliminating Step Budget Waste:

Standard optimization recipes assume long training budgets. Under 2,000 steps, a 100–200 step warmup wastes 5–10% of total training time at low learning rates. Removing warmup, cosine decay, and regularization allowed full-gradient utilization on every single step.

2. Solving the Real Bottleneck (Sequence Efficiency):

Devanagari text consumes 3 bytes per character in UTF-8. A standard byte tokenizer reduces a 128-token context window to only ~42 characters. By assigning single token IDs to frequent multi-byte characters, the custom tokenizer compressed the corpus from 7.3M bytes to 5.7M tokens (~22% reduction), allowing the model to see drastically more semantic content per context window.

3. Empirically Validated Superior Metric:

By switching the information representation channel rather than tuning model weights, the development BPB dropped from 2.3718 to 2.3136, achieving the best metric performance within all parameter and step constraints.
